Party Ideas

10 Epic Kids Party Ideas in Gaborone for 2025

February 20, 2025 By Avram Kids 6 min read

Gaborone is a vibrant city with a tight-knit community spirit, and nothing brings families together quite like a well-thrown kids party. Whether you're planning a birthday bash, an end-of-year celebration or just a fun summer get-together, 2025 is the year to do it properly.

We've rounded up the 10 best kids party ideas in Gaborone — from classic jumping castle setups to creative themed events that will have children talking for months.

1. Jumping Castle Garden Party

The timeless classic — and for very good reason. A jumping castle in your Gaborone garden is the single most effective way to keep 20+ children entertained for hours. Pair it with a picnic setup, juice station and snack table and you've got a complete party that costs a fraction of a venue hire.

Avram Kids tip: Book our Tropical Palm Castle for vibrant, eye-catching fun. It's available in multiple sizes to suit any garden.

2. Water Slide Summer Splash Party

Gaborone summers are hot. Use it to your advantage. A water slide hire transforms any backyard or open lawn into a mini water park — and costs a tiny fraction of a resort day out. Kids from ages 5 to 14 absolutely love it, and parents appreciate the shade and relaxed atmosphere.

Set up a table with towels, sunscreen and cold juice nearby. Add some inflatable pool toys and float rings for extra flair. This is one of the most Instagram-worthy party setups you can create in Gaborone.

3. Bounce & Splash Combo Extravaganza

Can't decide between a castle and a water slide? Don't choose — book both in one with a combo unit. Avram Kids' combo units feature a bouncing area, a slide and a water splash pool all in one inflatable. It's our most popular hire for a reason: children simply never want to leave.

This works brilliantly for parties with a wide age range — older kids dominate the slide while younger ones bounce safely in the castle section.

4. Tropical Beach Theme Party

Transform your Gaborone garden into a tropical paradise. Pair a water slide or combo inflatable with:

  • Inflatable flamingos and pool rings
  • Tropical fruit platters and coconut smoothies
  • Tiki torches and palm leaf decorations
  • Beach towels and water pistols as party favours
  • Hawaiian shirts as a dress code

The warm Botswana weather does the rest. This theme works spectacularly with our water slide units.

5. Sports Day Party

Perfect for energetic kids who love competition. Organise a mini sports day in your garden or at a local park in Gaborone, and include a jumping castle as the star attraction. Set up stations:

  • Sack race
  • Egg and spoon race
  • Tug of war
  • Jumping castle bounce competition
  • Obstacle course

Award medals or ribbon prizes for each event and crown an overall champion. Kids love the structure and the friendly competition.

6. Movie Night Under the Stars

Gaborone's clear night skies make outdoor movie screenings magical. Set up a projector screen in the garden, lay out bean bags and blankets, and add a jumping castle earlier in the day as the pre-show entertainment. Kids can burn off energy before settling in for the movie.

Choose a family favourite like The Lion King, Encanto or Moana. Serve popcorn, hot chocolate (in cooler months) and ice pops. Simple, affordable and deeply memorable.

7. DIY Craft Party with Inflatable Fun

Creative kids will love this combination. Set up a craft table inside (or under a shaded gazebo) with art supplies, and a jumping castle outside for physical play breaks. Rotate children between crafting and bouncing in groups.

Children take home their artwork as party favours — personal, meaningful and zero waste. It's a winner for smaller groups of 8–15 children.

8. Superhero Party

Ask children to arrive dressed as their favourite superhero — and turn your garden into a superhero training academy. Pair a large combo inflatable with:

  • Obstacle course "training" stations
  • Superhero-themed cake and cupcakes
  • Cape decoration activity at the craft table
  • A superhero themed photo wall

The jumping castle becomes the "superhero launching pad" — and suddenly every bounce feels like flying. Children aged 4–10 go wild for this concept.

9. Pool Party with Inflatable Add-Ons

If you or a friend has a pool in Gaborone, elevate the classic pool party with Avram Kids' inflatable slip-and-slide. Set up the slide to launch directly into the pool (where depth allows) or alongside it for splash zone action.

Add floating pool inflatables, music and a barbecue setup for the parents. This is one of the most enjoyable party formats for both children and adults, and works brilliantly across the hot season in Botswana.

10. Neighbourhood Kids Block Party

For something truly special, team up with two or three neighbouring families to co-host a block party on a quiet residential road or communal garden. Pool resources for:

  • A large combo inflatable (shared cost makes it very affordable per family)
  • Shared food and drinks
  • Music and a small dance floor
  • Tables and seating for parents to socialise

The children get an event that feels enormous and spectacular. Parents get a social gathering. Everyone wins. This is one of the most underutilised party formats in Gaborone — and it works brilliantly.
