Safety Tips for Inflatable Rentals in Botswana: What Every Parent Should Know

March 15, 2025 By Avram Kids 4 min read

Jumping castles and water slides are, without doubt, some of the most thrilling fun available for children at parties and events in Botswana. But like any physical activity, inflatable use comes with safety considerations that every parent should understand.

At Avram Kids, safety is not an afterthought — it's our first priority. We take extensive measures to ensure every inflatable we hire out is safe, clean and properly maintained. But great inflatable safety is a partnership: here's what parents and supervisors should know to keep every child safe during their inflatable hire experience in Gaborone.

Why Inflatable Safety Matters

Most inflatable injuries occur not because of equipment failure, but because of improper use or inadequate supervision. The good news is that the vast majority of incidents are entirely preventable with the right knowledge and attentive oversight.

By choosing a reputable inflatable hire company in Botswana — one that maintains its equipment properly and provides clear safety guidelines — you've already taken the most important step.

Before the Children Start Bouncing: Setup Checklist

Before you allow children onto any inflatable, run through this quick safety checklist:

  • ✅ Check the inflation: The castle should be fully inflated and firm. A soft, under-inflated unit is a safety concern — contact the hire company immediately.
  • ✅ Inspect anchor points: Ensure all anchor pegs or weights are properly secured. The inflatable should not move or shift in normal wind conditions.
  • ✅ Check for sharp objects: Remove any rocks, sticks, garden furniture or debris from around and underneath the inflatable area.
  • ✅ Verify cleanliness: The interior of the castle should be clean and dry (unless it's a water slide). At Avram Kids, we sanitise every unit before delivery.
  • ✅ Check the entrance/exit: The entrance flap should be accessible and free of obstructions for easy entry and exit.

Supervision Rules for Parents

Active adult supervision is the single most important safety measure at any inflatable event. Here are our key supervision guidelines:

  • Never leave children unsupervised on a jumping castle or water slide — not even briefly.
  • Designate a supervisor whose only job during the inflatable session is to watch the children. This person should not be distracted by food, phone or conversation.
  • Set clear rules before children start — no pushing, no flipping, no climbing the walls, one person on the slide at a time.
  • Separate age groups: Keep toddlers away from older, heavier children. The size and weight difference dramatically increases injury risk during collisions.

Key rule: The recommended maximum number of children is usually stated on the inflatable itself. At Avram Kids, we advise on the appropriate number for each unit at time of delivery.

Rules for Children on the Inflatable

Post these rules visibly or explain them clearly to all children before they start bouncing:

  1. No shoes inside the inflatable — socks or bare feet only.
  2. No sharp objects — remove glasses, hair clips, jewellery and belts.
  3. No food or drinks inside the castle.
  4. No flips or somersaults — especially for younger children.
  5. No pushing or rough play.
  6. One child at a time on water slides — wait for the previous child to clear the splash pool.
  7. Exit carefully — don't jump out of the entrance, step out gently.

Water Slide Safety Specifics

Water slides require additional care beyond standard jumping castle guidelines:

  • Always have an adult stationed at the splash pool — children can slip when wet and the ground around the pool will also be wet.
  • Non-swimmers should only use water slides when the splash pool is very shallow and an adult is within arm's reach.
  • Children should slide feet-first only — never head-first.
  • Keep the area around the water slide clear of obstacles when a child is coming down.
  • Be aware that wet surfaces around the inflatable can be slippery — discourage running near the unit.

Weather Considerations in Botswana

Botswana's climate brings specific weather considerations for outdoor inflatable use:

  • Heat: In hot months, the inflatable surface can become warm. Ensure children have regular water breaks and take rest periods in the shade. Water slides are ideal in the heat for this reason.
  • Wind: Light wind is generally fine. In strong or gusting wind conditions, the inflatable should be deflated. Our team will advise you on safe wind thresholds at time of setup.
  • Rain: Avoid use during heavy rain or electrical storms. Light drizzle is acceptable on water slides but can make non-water castles slippery.
  • Sun: Set up in a shaded area where possible, or ensure children use sunscreen. Long sessions in direct Gaborone sun can lead to sunburn and overheating.

Emergency Preparedness

While injuries are rare with proper supervision, it's good practice to be prepared:

  • Have a basic first aid kit nearby at all events involving physical activity.
  • Know the location of the nearest medical facility in your area of Gaborone.
  • Have our contact number saved (+267 77 997 996) so you can reach us if you have any concerns during the hire.
  • If the inflatable deflates unexpectedly, immediately remove all children and contact us — do not attempt to reinflate without guidance.

The Bottom Line

Jumping castle and water slide hire in Botswana is a fantastic, safe activity when handled correctly. By choosing a quality hire company, supervising children actively and following the guidelines above, you can give your kids a magical, memorable experience with complete confidence in their safety.
